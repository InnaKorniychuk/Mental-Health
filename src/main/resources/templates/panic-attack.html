Self-Calming Techniques

Box (“Square”) Breathing

Try to relax your muscles as much as possible and focus on your breathing. Slowly exhale, then perform four breathing actions: inhale, hold, exhale, and hold again. Mentally count to four during each step. Repeat this “square” breathing cycle until you feel calmer.

Body Awareness Exercises

  • Touch and rub the tip of your nose;
  • Stick your tongue out as far as possible, trying to reach your chin;
  • Rub your body from feet to head, then in reverse;
  • Pat yourself with your palms or throw a blanket over yourself to feel your body boundaries;
  • Rub the points between your fingers;
  • Without turning your head, try looking to the side;
  • Rinse your mouth with water and gently press your lips together.

Objects in a Crowd

If a panic attack occurs in a crowd, find about five people wearing hats, similar clothing, or look for objects of a specific color. Name them out loud — this will help bring your attention back to reality.

Grounding

Look around and identify:

  • 5 objects you can see;
  • 4 objects you can touch;
  • 3 objects you can hear;
  • 2 objects you can smell;
  • 1 object you can taste.

Then take several slow inhales and exhales.

Wall Anchoring

Approach a wall and press your shoulders, hips, and heels against it simultaneously. Focus on these points, aligning your body.
